Understanding Phishing Threats

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where criminals attempt to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information by posing as legitimate organizations or contacts.

Common Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

The most common type where attackers send fraudulent emails impersonating legitimate organizations. They often create urgency and request immediate action.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks that use personal information to make emails appear more legitimate. Often directed at specific individuals or organizations.

Real-World Example Analysis

Anatomy of a Phishing Email

Red Flags to Notice:

  • Mismatched sender email (help-desk@roadrunner.cc instead of mercer.edu)
  • Urgency and threat of account termination
  • Suspicious link URL different from displayed text
  • Generic greeting ("Dear User")
  • Spelling and grammatical errors

How to Protect Yourself

Verify Sender

Always check the actual email address, not just the display name. Hover over links to see the true destination.

Don't Rush

Be suspicious of urgent requests. Legitimate organizations rarely demand immediate action via email.

Use Official Channels

Instead of clicking links, manually type known URLs or call official phone numbers to verify requests.
